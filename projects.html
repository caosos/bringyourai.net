Domain Recovery Notes

If a site was built on Emergent or Base44 and the owned domain no longer works, this is the likely failure chain.

Owned domain ≠ deployed site. The domain can be registered and paid for while the app target is broken, deleted, renamed, or disconnected.
DNS may still point to the old platform. If CAOS Connect or CAOS Trainings were tied to Emergent, their CNAME/A records may point to a platform target that no longer serves the app.
Platform previews can survive while custom domains fail. A Base44 or Emergent preview URL can work even when the custom domain is not connected.
SSL/certificate provisioning can block the site. Even correct DNS can fail if the platform has not issued or renewed HTTPS for the custom domain.
Recovery path: inventory every domain, identify registrar/DNS host, identify intended app/repo, inspect DNS records, then choose whether to repoint to GitHub Pages, owned server, Base44, or a new deployment target.
