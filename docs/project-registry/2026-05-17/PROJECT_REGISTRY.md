## Registry thesis

Most project ideas in this ecosystem are not separate inventions. They are vertical expressions of one root architecture:

> Persistent contextual intelligence layered onto existing people, tools, devices, buildings, workflows, institutions, and machines.

This registry separates the ideas into clean project families so they can be shown publicly, prioritized, and developed without drift.

## Project family map

### P0 — CAOS Core Platform

The root system architecture. CAOS provides the memory, governance, orchestration, context, receipts, explicit learning discipline, and tool-connected runtime model that all other projects depend on.

**Dependency role:** Everything else depends on this architecture.

### P1 — CAOS Care

The first practical vertical: senior-care support, resident assistance, reminders, staff workflow support, safety signals, documentation, family/staff visibility, and wearable/tablet/voice interfaces.

**Commercial reason:** Closest to Michael's real-world environment and immediate need.

### P1 — Bring Your AI Command Layer

The umbrella public identity and broad product thesis: users should be able to bring their AI, memory, preferences, workflows, devices, and context across platforms instead of being trapped inside disconnected apps.

**Public reason:** This is the resume/portfolio surface and broadest story.

### P2 — Retrofit Building + Legacy Machinery Layer

A drop-in intelligence layer for old buildings, old machines, and legacy systems. The goal is not to replace the world; it is to add AI-guided troubleshooting, command, monitoring, and workflow assistance to what already exists.

**Strategic reason:** Most real infrastructure is old. Retrofit is more realistic than replacement.

### P2 — Hardware / Sensor / Wearable Layer

The physical input/output layer: pendants, tablets, microphones, RF receivers, sensors, gateways, smart-home/building devices, and rugged or medical-adjacent hardware.

**Strategic reason:** CAOS/CAOS Care becomes more useful when connected to real-world signals and devices.

### P3 — Clinical Safety Support Layer

A supervised assistive layer for doctors, nurses, and caregivers that helps catch possible medication conflicts, allergy risks, order inconsistencies, and documentation gaps.

**Boundary:** Must be positioned as support and risk-flagging, not autonomous diagnosis or medical authority.

### P3 — 911 + Troubleshooting Support Layer

A guided escalation layer for urgent problems, support workflows, equipment failures, and emergency triage routing. It can help users collect facts, follow steps, route alerts, and preserve incident receipts.

**Boundary:** Must not replace emergency services or professional judgment.

### P4 — Education / IEP Profile Layer

A student-centered adaptive support layer that preserves individualized learning context, accommodations, strengths, needs, history, and practical supports for teachers, parents, and students.

**Boundary:** Needs strong privacy and school-system alignment before public build.

## Canonical principle

Do not present every idea as a separate company. Present them as project families under one architecture:

- BringYourAI = public umbrella / identity / portability thesis.
- CAOS = core operating intelligence.
- CAOS Care = first vertical.
- Retrofit + hardware layers = deployment/enabling layers.
- Education, clinical, 911 = later verticals.
